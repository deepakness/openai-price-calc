Germany Mulls ChatGPT Ban Over Privacy Concerns, Following Italy's Lead

April 7, 2023

Germany is considering a ban on the AI chatbot ChatGPT due to potential privacy violations, according to local reports. The German data protection chief, Ulrich Kelber, mentioned in an interview with Handelsblatt newspaper that their regulators have been in talks with Italian authorities since Italy banned ChatGPT last week. He stated that a similar ban in Germany is a possibility.

The Italian ban on ChatGPT came after the country's data protection authority, GPDP, determined that the platform lacked appropriate legal grounds to collect personal data of its users. ChatGPT, developed by OpenAI, gathers some data to improve its AI algorithm that produces responses.

Italy's data protection body also criticized OpenAI for not implementing age verification checks, potentially exposing young users to unsuitable content. The GPDP argued that there is no legal justification for the extensive collection and storage of personal information for the purpose of training the platform's algorithms.

As concerns mount, data protection regulators from other European countries, including France and Ireland, have been in discussions with Italy's data protection agency. A spokesperson for Ireland's Data Protection Commissioner (DPC) stated they would follow up with the Italian regulator to understand their rationale and coordinate with all EU data protection authorities on the matter.

Italy was the first Western country to ban ChatGPT; however, the app is unavailable in several countries, including China, Iran, North Korea, and Russia. European consumer organization BEUC has urged the European Union to investigate the risks posed by ChatGPT and similar chatbots to European consumers.

OpenAI has not commented on the ban but has blocked ChatGPT in Italy and ceased processing data from Italian users.
